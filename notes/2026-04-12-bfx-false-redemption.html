After some miserable weeks, BNK FearX broke their loss streak today in a 2-0 win vs DNS. Before the match even begins, my prediction of a roster change was fulfilled, just not in the right position strangely. VicLa was subbed out for Daystar, he is not playing in the Challengers league, instead he sits on the bench presumably playing scrims sometimes. I don't think this change mattered gameplay-wise, but perhaps mentally it did change things.

DNS is an inconsistent team, especially with Pyosik, but the biggest stinker of the series may have been DuDu. Game 1, DuDu has last pick for some devious counter to Clear's most prioritized blind; Renekton. Gnar was deemed too conventional, too played out to the innovative staff and top laner of DN SOOPers, they reinvent the meta by picking Zaahen! The champ CAN scale to become a menace, but his immobility and need for long fighting makes this last pick dumbfoundingly pointless.

BFX had thrown a significant lead in the game that almost assured me they would continue their loss streak, but a surprisingly bad teamfight from DNS counteracted it. Let's watch it:

Firstly, drake is spawning in 1 minute, marking this fight in extremely dangerous territory. Let's say DNS wins the fight overwhelmingly using all their summoners and their ultimate abilities while BFX do not use all of them. DNS is near-sighted though, they burn every spell and miss most while also dying. Notice how DuDu instantly combusts as the immobile pseudo-tank?

I'm not going to analyze game 2 since they gave Ashe Seraphine and picked Jhin.

I'm also not going to reward BFX too much for this win. I don't think Daystar is a good player, and all this series does is input false data for further mediocrity. Props to Kellin though for a supernatural Neeko performance, usually one of his worst champs statistically.
